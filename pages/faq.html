Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about PLS 120

Course Logistics

Q: What are the prerequisites for this course?

A: You need to have completed either MAT 16A or MAT 21A (calculus). Basic algebra and mathematical reasoning skills are essential for success in this course.

Q: Do I need prior programming experience?

A: No prior programming experience is required. We will start with the basics of R programming and gradually build up your skills throughout the course.

Q: What if I miss a class?

A: All lecture materials and recordings (when available) will be posted on the course website. However, class participation is part of your grade, so regular attendance is important. Contact the instructor if you have an extended absence.

Q: Can I use other statistical software instead of R?

A: R is the required software for this course. All tutorials, assignments, and examples are designed for R. Learning R is also valuable for your future career in agricultural sciences.

Technical Issues

Q: I'm having trouble installing R/RStudio. What should I do?

A: First, make sure you install R before installing RStudio. If you continue to have problems:

  • Check that you downloaded the correct version for your operating system
  • Try RStudio Cloud as an alternative (no installation required)
  • Visit office hours for hands-on help
  • Contact IT support if it's a computer-specific issue

Q: My R code isn't working. How can I get help?

A: When seeking help with code:

  1. Copy the exact error message
  2. Include the code that's causing the problem
  3. Describe what you expected to happen
  4. Post on the discussion forum or bring to office hours

Q: Can I work on assignments using my tablet or phone?

A: While RStudio Cloud can work on tablets, we strongly recommend using a computer with a full keyboard for coding assignments. The mobile experience is not optimal for statistical programming.

Statistical Concepts

Q: When should I use a t-test vs. ANOVA?

A: Use a t-test when comparing means between two groups. Use ANOVA when comparing means among three or more groups. If you have only two groups, both methods will give the same p-value.

Q: What does "statistical significance" really mean?

A: Statistical significance (typically p < 0.05) means that if there were truly no effect, you would see results this extreme or more extreme less than 5% of the time by chance alone. It doesn't necessarily mean the effect is practically important.

Q: How do I know if my data meets the assumptions for a particular test?

A: We'll cover assumption checking for each statistical test. Generally, you'll use:

  • Histograms and Q-Q plots to check normality
  • Residual plots to check homoscedasticity
  • Levene's test for equal variances
  • Scatterplots to check linearity (for regression)

Q: What should I do if my data doesn't meet test assumptions?

A: Options include:

  • Transform your data (log, square root, etc.)
  • Use non-parametric alternatives
  • Use robust statistical methods
  • Collect more data if sample size is small

Career and Applications

Q: How will I use statistics in my agricultural career?

A: Statistics are essential in agriculture for:

  • Designing and analyzing field experiments
  • Quality control in food production
  • Environmental monitoring and assessment
  • Economic analysis of farming practices
  • Research and development of new varieties/methods
  • Regulatory compliance and reporting

Q: Should I continue with more advanced statistics courses?

A: If you're planning graduate school or a research career, additional statistics courses are highly recommended. Consider:

  • PLS 205: Experimental Design
  • STA 135: Multivariate Data Analysis
  • STA 141: Statistical Computing
  • STA 108: Regression Analysis

Q: What other software should I learn besides R?

A: R is excellent and widely used, but you might also encounter:

  • SAS (common in industry and government)
  • Python (growing in popularity for data science)
  • SPSS (user-friendly for basic analyses)
  • Excel (still widely used, though limited)

Study Tips

Q: How should I study for exams?

A: Effective study strategies include:

  • Practice problems from homework and class activities
  • Create summary sheets of key formulas and concepts
  • Form study groups to discuss difficult concepts
  • Use the practice datasets to try different analyses
  • Attend review sessions and office hours

Q: I'm struggling with the mathematical concepts. What can I help?

A: Don't panic! Try these approaches:

  • Focus on understanding concepts rather than memorizing formulas
  • Use visual aids and real examples to make concepts concrete
  • Visit the Learning Skills Center for tutoring
  • Form study groups with classmates
  • Come to office hours regularly, not just before exams

Q: How much time should I spend on this course each week?

A: Plan for about 8-10 hours per week outside of class, including:

  • Reading assignments (1-2 hours)
  • Homework assignments (3-4 hours)
  • Reviewing notes and practicing (2-3 hours)
  • Final project work (varies by week)
